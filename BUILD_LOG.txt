================================================================================
BUGS FOUND AND FIXED DURING BUILD
================================================================================

  1. CarRacing-v3 not available in gymnasium 0.29.1
     Fix: Used CarRacing-v2 (same env, different version string)

  2. GrayscaleObservation typo in gymnasium 0.29.1 API
     Fix: Used GrayScaleObservation (capital S)

  3. FrameStackObservation not in gymnasium 0.29.1
     Fix: Used FrameStack wrapper

  4. box2d-py SWIG float32 type error on Python 3.13
     Fix: Added Float64Action wrapper to cast actions before box2d

  5. pip-installed swig shadowing system swig binary
     Fix: Uninstalled pip swig, used system /usr/bin/swig

  6. Python stdout buffered when piped to file via nohup
     Fix: Set PYTHONUNBUFFERED=1 for training process

  7. Action space saturation (tanh on large raw outputs)
     Fix: Centered tanh scaling — act_center + act_scale * tanh(raw).
     mu_head gain=0.01 keeps raw outputs small, tanh operates in
     linear regime where gradients flow.

  8. log_std stuck at clamp boundary (not learnable)
     Fix: Widened clamp range to [-2.5, 0.5], initialized at -1.0
     (middle of range). std starts at 0.37, can learn to [0.08, 1.65].

  9. Value function clipping hurting learning
     Fix: Removed value clipping, used simple MSE loss. Value clipping
     is theoretically motivated but empirically often harmful.

  10. Too many gradient steps per rollout (n_epochs=10)
      Fix: Reduced to n_epochs=4. Over-optimization per rollout causes
      policy to drift too far, triggering KL early stopping constantly.

  KL divergence: Stayed below target_kl (0.02) for most updates.
  Occasional spikes triggered early stopping appropriately.

  Reward normalization: RunningMeanStd (scale only, no shift) kept
  rewards in a stable range for the value network throughout training.

  Learning rate: Linear decay from 2.5e-4 to 2.5e-5 (10% floor).
  Floor prevents learning from halting in late training.

  The bimodal reward distribution (some episodes ~0, others ~870)
  is characteristic of CarRacing: procedurally generated tracks mean
  some layouts are harder. The car occasionally goes off-track on
  tight turns but completes most tracks at near-human level.

================================================================================
INTERVIEW TALKING POINTS
================================================================================

  1. Why PPO over DQN?
     Continuous action space — DQN needs discrete actions. PPO uses
     Gaussian policy for natural continuous control.

  2. What does GAE do?
     Balances bias vs variance in advantage estimation. Lambda=0.95
     gives low-bias estimates at cost of higher variance. Without GAE,
     you're stuck choosing between high-bias (TD) or high-variance (MC).

  3. Why frame stacking?
     Single frame has no motion info. 4 stacked frames give implicit
     velocity/acceleration, restoring the Markov property.

  4. What does entropy bonus prevent?
     Premature policy collapse. Without it, the policy can converge
     to a narrow distribution too early, getting stuck in local optima.

  5. Why 8 parallel envs?
     Decorrelated samples for PPO. Single-env rollouts are highly
     correlated (sequential states). 8 envs give diverse experience
     per batch, stabilizing training.

  6. Hardest bug: action space saturation. The tanh/sigmoid squashing
     caused gradient saturation when raw mu_head outputs grew large.
     Solved with centered tanh scaling (act_center + act_scale * tanh)
     combined with small gain (0.01) orthogonal init on mu_head to
     keep outputs in the linear regime of tanh.

  7. Key tuning insight: log_std initialization and clamp range matter
     enormously. Initializing at 0 (std=1.0) is too exploratory for
     CarRacing's narrow action ranges. Starting at -1.0 (std=0.37)
     with room to learn in both directions was critical.
